Life Expectancy Prediction

Machine Learning for Health & Demographic Analysis

A comprehensive machine learning project that predicts life expectancy based on various health, economic, and social factors using WHO data. This model analyzes relationships between lifestyle, healthcare quality, and longevity across different countries to provide insights into global health patterns.

Python Scikit-learn Pandas WHO Data Health Analytics Regression Models

Project Overview

This project leverages machine learning techniques to predict life expectancy across different countries using comprehensive health and demographic data from the World Health Organization (WHO). The model analyzes multiple factors including healthcare expenditure, immunization coverage, mortality rates, and socioeconomic indicators to understand their impact on population longevity.
By implementing various regression algorithms and feature engineering techniques, this project provides valuable insights into the factors that most significantly influence life expectancy, helping policymakers and health organizations make data-driven decisions to improve public health outcomes.

Key Features

Multi-Factor Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of health, economic, and social factors affecting life expectancy across 193 countries.

Predictive Modeling

Advanced regression models with feature selection and hyperparameter tuning for optimal prediction accuracy.

Global Insights

Cross-country analysis revealing regional patterns and disparities in health outcomes and longevity.

Feature Engineering

Sophisticated data preprocessing and feature engineering to extract maximum predictive value from raw data.

Data Sources & Variables

The dataset comprises 22 key variables from WHO and UN data spanning from 2000-2015, covering 193 countries with comprehensive health and demographic indicators.
Health Indicators

Adult mortality, infant deaths, alcohol consumption, hepatitis B and measles immunization rates

Economic Factors

GDP per capita, health expenditure as percentage of GDP, government health spending

Demographic Data

Population statistics, HIV/AIDS prevalence, thinness indicators for different age groups

Social Indicators

Schooling years, BMI statistics, polio immunization coverage, diphtheria vaccination rates

The final model demonstrates excellent predictive performance across multiple evaluation metrics, successfully capturing the complex relationships between various factors and life expectancy.

Key Insights

Major Findings

  • Adult Mortality: Strongest predictor with highest correlation to life expectancy
  • HIV/AIDS Impact: Significant negative correlation, especially in Sub-Saharan Africa
  • Education Effect: Years of schooling show strong positive correlation with longevity
  • Economic Factors: GDP and health expenditure moderately correlate with life expectancy
  • Immunization: Vaccination coverage shows positive but varying impact by region
  • Infant Health: Infant deaths strongly inversely correlated with life expectancy
  • Regional Patterns: Developed countries cluster with higher life expectancy values
  • BMI Relationship: Non-linear relationship requiring careful feature engineering

Methodology

The project follows a comprehensive data science workflow including data cleaning, exploratory analysis, feature engineering, model selection, and validation. Multiple regression algorithms were tested including Linear Regression, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and Support Vector Regression.

Implementation Steps

  • Comprehensive data cleaning and missing value imputation
  • Exploratory data analysis with correlation and statistical testing
  • Feature engineering including polynomial features and interaction terms
  • Model selection through cross-validation and grid search
  • Feature importance analysis and model interpretability
  • Performance evaluation on holdout test set
  • Residual analysis and model diagnostic validation
  • Visualization of predictions vs. actual values by region
